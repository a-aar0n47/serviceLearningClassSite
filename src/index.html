About Us

Discover why we do service learning in Aga Khan.

Our Mission

The Service Learning class aims to develop students' research, public speaking, writing, and teamwork skills while promoting environmental awareness. Students will create skits, write blogs, poems, and articles, and work on group projects that advocate for sustainability within both the school and broader community. By the end of the term, students will educate 500 people on five environmental issues, organize two school-wide events showcasing their performances, and publish their creative and informational content on the school’s website and social media. These efforts will enhance their academic abilities and encourage environmental responsibility.

Our Vision

In our service learning class, we aim to create meaningful change in our community while deepening our understanding of critical social issues. By combining classroom knowledge with hands-on projects, we hope to address real-world challenges and develop the skills needed to lead with empathy and collaboration. Whether we focus on environmental sustainability, educational outreach, or social justice, our goal is to actively contribute to society’s betterment. Through this work, we seek to grow as compassionate, responsible citizens of Uganda, recognizing that each of us can make a difference and inspire others to give back as well.

Importance

Service learning holds immense significance as it seamlessly integrates academic theory with practical application, enabling students to translate classroom knowledge into impactful, real-world experiences. It fosters a nuanced understanding of complex social issues, while promoting critical thinking and empathy through active community engagement. By participating in service learning, students refine essential skills such as leadership, collaboration, and strategic problem-solving, while cultivating a profound sense of civic responsibility. This pedagogical approach not only accelerates personal and intellectual development but also empowers students to become agents of meaningful change, contributing to societal advancement on both local and global scales.
